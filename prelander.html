Your Contacts Are Harboring Protein Residue & Other Grossness

Did you know your cornea is the only part of your body that receives oxygen directly from the air? Contact lenses are built with microscopic pores — like a tiny net — to let that oxygen flow through.

Those tiny pores are exactly the problem. Every time you wear contact lenses, protein deposits and microscopic debris from your eyes start attaching to them. This buildup doesn't just sit on the surface — it seeps deep into the lens material, making it nearly impossible to remove with rubbing, rinsing, or soaking alone.

These proteins block oxygen flow, causing your eyes to dry out faster — leading to irritation, redness, and even infections.

Worse: the buildup creates a rough surface that becomes a magnet for bacteria, turning your lenses into a breeding ground for unwanted microorganisms and hidden threats.
